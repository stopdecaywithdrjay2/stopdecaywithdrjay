When Plaque builds up, there is an increased risk of decay and gum disease. Chewing can become impaired, and can create stress on other teeth as well as the jaw.

Cheeks can sink in and the face can change appearance while lips will thin and the chin will just out causing pouches to appear on the sides of the jaw. Also, if back teeth are not replaced, the join used in chewing can be damaged causing pain upon eating and speaking.

Crowns are also known as caps, and are coverings used to restore broken, discolored or badly decayed teeth.

Bridges are tooth replacements that are attached to adjoining natural teeth. They work for people who have only a few missing teeth. They require that the adjoining teeth be healthy and have adequate gum and root support.
